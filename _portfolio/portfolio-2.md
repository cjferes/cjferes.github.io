We aim to enable user scheduling for massive wireless systems, in order to improve spectral efficiency at reasonable computational cost. To ensure spatial diversity of MIMO networks for resource-sharing users, we leverage the capabilities of unsupervised learning over Riemannian manifolds to discard user pairs with high co-channel interference, to then exploit these learned features in the scheduling stage. Our results with simple schemes show the scalability of this approach when considering thousand of users, in both downlink and uplink systems. 

Developing a new user scheduling approach to improve co-channel interference, based on clustering similar user channels via Riemannian unsupervised learning and assign them into distinct resource sharing groups.
