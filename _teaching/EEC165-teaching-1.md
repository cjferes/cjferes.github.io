The course covers the principles of wireless communication systems: transmission and reception of signals over a channel; statistical characterization of signals and noise; principles of operation and digital transceiver PHY design. The course also has a hands-on lab where students design point-to-point PHY transceivers using software-defined radios, with the final project being the implementation of a bare-bones 802.11a PHY radio.

I was the instructor of the course during 2019-2021, supervised by Prof. Zhi Ding. I experienced both in-person and remote instruction. I was also the Teaching Assistant of the class in 2017 and 2018, where with Prof. Ding we redesigned the lab contents, sessions and project.
